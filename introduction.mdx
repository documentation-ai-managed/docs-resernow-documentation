## Core Features

ReserNow offers comprehensive booking management capabilities that go beyond traditional scheduling systems. You manage appointments across multiple locations, handle complex availability rules, and use AI to optimize your booking processes.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Appointment Management" icon="calendar">
    You create and manage appointments with real-time availability checking. The system automatically handles conflicts and sends notifications to customers and staff.

    <Callout kind="info">Appointments can be booked up to 6 months in advance with customizable booking windows.</Callout>

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Create Service" icon="plus">
        You define services with duration, pricing, and resource requirements.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Set Availability" icon="clock">
        You configure staff schedules and business hours.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Book Appointment" icon="check">
        Customers select dates and times through your booking interface.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Resource Allocation" icon="users">
    You allocate staff and equipment to appointments efficiently. The AI engine suggests optimal assignments based on skills and availability.

    <CodeGroup tabs="JavaScript,Python">

## Integration Capabilities

You connect ReserNow with your existing tools and platforms. Webhooks notify you of booking changes, API endpoints allow custom integrations, and Zapier support enables no-code connections.

| Integration Type | Description | Setup Time |
|------------------|-------------|------------|
| Webhooks | Real-time notifications | `<5 minutes` |
| API | Custom integrations | `15-30 minutes` |
| Zapier | No-code workflows | `<2 minutes` |

<ExpandableGroup>
  <Expandable title="Webhook Setup Guide" default-open="false">
    You configure webhooks to receive notifications when bookings are created, updated, or cancelled. This keeps your systems synchronized in real-time.

    First, you navigate to the webhooks section in your dashboard. Then you add a new webhook endpoint with your target URL. Finally, you select the events you want to monitor.
